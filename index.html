Muscle Group Map

⚡How Muscles Actually Work

Brain sends signal → motor neuron → muscle fibres → actin & myosin filaments slide → contraction. This is the Sliding Filament Theory. Contraction strength depends on number of fibres recruited, speed of signal, and training adaptation. Beginner strength gains are largely neurological — your brain gets better at recruiting fibres before muscle size increases.

🔬2 Types of Muscle Growth
MYOFIBRILLAR

Increases muscle fibre density. Result: strength + hardness. Low reps, heavy weight (3–6 reps).

SARCOPLASMIC

Increases muscle volume and fluid. Result: size + pump. Moderate reps (8–15 reps).

PPL program trains both — compounds build strength, accessories build size.

Key Metabolic Fact

Each kg of muscle burns approximately 10–15 kcal/day at rest. More muscle = higher basal metabolic rate = easier fat loss long-term. This is why strength training is non-negotiable during a cut.

Why Protein Matters

2g
per kg bodyweight · daily minimum
At 76kg → target ~152g protein/day. This preserves muscle while in a calorie deficit.
🔬Muscle Protein Synthesis (MPS)

Every time you eat protein, your body triggers MPS — the cellular process of building and repairing muscle fibres. The trigger is Leucine — you need ~2.5–3g leucine per meal to maximally stimulate MPS. High-quality proteins like whey, eggs, and chicken hit this threshold easily. Plant proteins often don't — combine sources or supplement.

⏰Protein Distribution Strategy

Don't eat all your protein in one meal. Spread across 3–5 meals, each containing 25–40g protein. This creates repeated MPS spikes throughout the day — maximising muscle retention during your cut. Daily total matters most, but distribution optimises it.

🔥Thermic Effect of Protein

Your body burns 20–30% of protein calories just digesting it. Eat 200kcal of chicken — you only absorb ~150kcal net. Carbs burn 5–10%, fats burn 3%. High protein intake automatically creates a small daily deficit through digestion alone.

🍽️Satiety — Protein Kills Hunger

Protein is the most satiating macronutrient. 30g protein keeps you full 2x longer than 30g carbs. Critical in a deficit when hunger is your biggest enemy. Lead every meal with protein.

Fat Loss Science

The Only Law That Matters
Calories In < Calories Out = Fat Loss
Everything else — keto, fasting, clean eating — only works because it creates a calorie deficit. Period.
⚡How Fat is Actually Burned

Fat burn is hormonal: Low insulin → hormone signal → triglycerides break into fatty acids → enter bloodstream → used for energy (oxidation). Stubborn belly fat has higher alpha receptors and lower blood flow — it's the last to go. No shortcut. Consistent deficit + time is the only solution.

🔢The 7,700 Rule

1kg of fat = 7,700 kcal stored. A 500 kcal/day deficit = 3,500 kcal/week = ~0.45kg lost per week. At your stats, this is the target. Consistency over 8 weeks = 3.5kg+ gone. Math doesn't lie.

📉Metabolic Adaptation (Why Plateaus Happen)

Your body fights fat loss. As you diet, your TDEE drops — from 2,200 kcal to ~2,000 kcal after weeks. This is adaptive thermogenesis. You also move less subconsciously. Causes: less movement, lower energy output, hormonal changes (leptin drops, ghrelin rises). Fix: increase steps, slight calorie adjustment, or a 1–2 week diet break at maintenance.

😤Cortisol & Belly Fat

Chronic stress spikes cortisol — your catabolic stress hormone. Elevated cortisol breaks down muscle, increases belly fat storage, and disrupts sleep. Control via: adequate sleep, not overtraining, and stress management. This is why recovery is as important as training.

🚶NEAT — Your Hidden Weapon

Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis — all movement outside gym. Walking, fidgeting, daily activity. NEAT often burns more calories than your gym workout. Target 8,000–12,000 steps daily. This alone can create a 300–500 kcal/day extra deficit without gym time.

⚖️Why the Scale Lies

Water retention, food weight, glycogen, and hormones cause 1–3kg of daily fluctuation. Never judge by one weigh-in. Track weekly averages. The trend over 2–3 weeks is the truth. Ideal rate: 0.5–1% of bodyweight per week.

Sleep & Recovery

8h
minimum for muscle growth & fat loss
Sleep Cycle Stages
Deep Sleep (Stage 3–4) — 90% of growth hormone released here. Muscle repair happens NOW.
REM Sleep — Memory consolidation, motor learning, neural recovery. Critical for skill retention.
Light Sleep — Transition phases. Less anabolic.
😤Cortisol — Sleep Deprivation's Weapon

6h sleep vs 8h sleep can increase cortisol by 37%. That cortisol breaks down muscle, stores fat (especially belly), kills testosterone. Skipping sleep is the fastest way to ruin your shred.

🍕Sleep & Hunger Hormones

Bad sleep raises ghrelin (hunger) and lowers leptin (fullness). You'll feel 20–25% hungrier the next day and crave high-calorie foods. This destroys more diets than anything else. Fix your sleep first.
